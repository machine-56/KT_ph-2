Experience Kerala

Village Life

Village life of God's Own Country is a symphony composed on strings of beauty painted with myriad traditions and cultures.

Kerala Cuisine

Spices have always played an integral part of Kerala's history. Our food is an example of the very same and we have offerings for people from all over the world.

Monsoon

Kerala has mainly two rainy seasons. The Southwest Monsoon that arrives in the month of June is called Edavappathy. Mid October witnesses the arrival of the Northeast Monsoon.

Historical places

Kerala has many fascinating tales from the past for you. In simpler words, the state is a treasure trove of historical monuments. Admire the grandeur of the ancient forts or visit the palaces.

Wildlife

Kerala is home to a wide variety of fauna. Its forests are home to more than 1000 species of trees and plants and some rare species of birds and other animals

Traditions

The culture of Kerala has developed over the past millennia, influences from other parts of India and abroad. It is defined by its antiquity and the organic continuity sustained by the Malayali people. Modern Kerala society took shape owing to migrations from different parts of India and abroad throughout Classical Antiquity.

Art-forms

The performing arts of God's Own Country have a special pull and connection amongst all audiences. On stage or by the road, the setting is never the primary attraction but rather, what steals the spotlight.

Kalari

Kalaripayattu is an Indian martial art and fighting style that originated in modern-day Kerala.

Beach

As Kerala is a coastal state, some of the most popular tourist destinations in Kerala are beaches like Kovam, Varkala, Chowara, Chavakkad, Nattika, Cherai, Kizhunna, Poovar and the list goes on and on.
